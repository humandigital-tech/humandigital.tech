Structural Rationale

Why separate for-profit
and nonprofit?

This structure is not incidental — it is the deliberate architecture of every successful universal identifier standard. The DOI Foundation, EIDR, ISNI, and ISO itself all operate on the same principle: a neutral, independently governed standard earns adoption across competing commercial interests in a way that a proprietary, company-owned standard never can.

For H&D
Commercially neutral standard = broader adoption
Studios, streamers, guilds, and agencies are more willing to adopt an identifier governed by a neutral nonprofit than one owned by a company with a commercial interest in their data. The nonprofit structure removes a major objection from enterprise sales conversations before it’s raised.
For Talent & Guilds
Trust without conflict of interest
SAG-AFTRA, WGA, DGA, and other guilds can engage with the HAND standard knowing it is not controlled by a party with a financial stake in their contract negotiations. The nonprofit governance structure is a precondition for guild trust — not a nice-to-have.
For Investors
Durable competitive moat
H&D’s commercial services are built on a standard that no competitor can simply replicate or acquire. The nonprofit structure protects the registry’s integrity and H&D’s position as the primary commercial services provider in the ecosystem. You are investing in the commercial layer of a standard — not just a product.
For the Industry
Precedent from trusted infrastructure
Every major identifier standard the media industry depends on — DOI, EIDR, ISRC, ISNI — is governed by a nonprofit or standards body. HAND follows that model precisely, ensuring interoperability and longevity that proprietary systems cannot guarantee. This is not an experiment. It is the proven model.

Common Questions

Frequently asked —
plainly answered.

Does H&D own the HAND standard?
No. H&D founded HAND and currently provides the primary commercial services built on it, but the HAND identifier standard is governed by an independent nonprofit. The registry is maintained through the DOI Foundation — the same infrastructure that underpins academic publishing and EIDR. H&D does not own, control, or have the ability to alter the standard’s governance unilaterally.
Which entity do I contract with?
All commercial contracts — MSAs, SOWs, service agreements, and investment instruments — are with Human & Digital, Inc., the for-profit Delaware C-Corp. HAND — The Universal Talent Identifier is the nonprofit standards body and is not a party to commercial agreements.
What happens to my HAND IDs if H&D’s commercial status changes?
HAND IDs are issued through the DOI Foundation registry and governed by the HAND nonprofit. They are permanently and freely resolvable regardless of any changes to H&D’s commercial operations. This is by design — the same permanence guarantee that makes DOI-based identifiers the trusted infrastructure for academic publishing and EIDR.
How does H&D make money if the standard is nonprofit?
H&D’s revenue comes from professional services — Identity Intelligence, HAND ID Registration and management, Meta-Tagging & Data Labeling, and Data Transformation — delivered to enterprise clients, agencies, and rights holders. The nonprofit standard creates the trusted infrastructure. H&D creates the commercial value layer on top of it. This is the same model as many standards-adjacent businesses across the technology and media industries.
Is HAND affiliated with any guild or union?
HAND is an independent nonprofit and is not affiliated with or controlled by any guild, union, studio, or platform. H&D actively engages with guilds, unions, and labor organizations across the Cultural Industries on the commercial services layer.
Is HAND interoperable with EIDR?
Yes — and the relationship is deeper than interoperability. EIDR and HAND are sibling DOI Registration Agencies operating on the same Handle System infrastructure. EIDR identifies content (films, TV shows, edits). HAND identifies talent (the people who create that content). A production record can carry both. Raymond Drewry, Co-Founder of EIDR and Principal Scientist at MovieLabs, sits on HAND’s board. HAND’s success deepens EIDR’s value — and vice versa.
