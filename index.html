Why Now

The media industry has solved content identification.
It has never solved talent identification.

The die has already been cast: AI, automation, and synthetic media are here, and they’re transforming how stories are made, distributed, and monetized. Likeness, voice, and performance can now be replicated, recombined, and deployed at machine speed.

But the systems governing consent, attribution, and authorization are still rooted in a slower world — contracts, emails, spreadsheets, institutional memory. The result is not just confusion or inefficiency. It’s risk. Reputational risk, legal risk, moral risk.

HAND exists because automation without trust breaks systems. The future of media cannot require choosing between scale and human agency. The real failure has been trying to scale without structure.

“Standards are how values endure. Infrastructure is how ethics scale.”

Force 01 — Legislative
CA AB 1836, AB 2602, NY S.8420-A, and the federal NO FAKES Act create structural economic incentive for studios, platforms, and agencies to adopt verifiable talent IDs. This is not anticipated regulation — it is active law.
Force 02 — Labor & CBA
SAG-AFTRA/AMPTP 2026 negotiations, MLBPA/MLB CBA expiration (December 2026), and active player association engagement across NFL, NBA, NHL, and international federations create an urgent need for neutral, portable talent identifiers.
Force 03 — AI Provenance
Synthetic media and AI-generated likenesses have made talent identification a trust and authenticity crisis. C2PA can attest the chain of custody of a file. HAND attests who is in it. Provenance without resolution is a label on an empty box.
Force 04 — NCAA NIL
The NCAA NIL era has created an identity verification and compliance infrastructure gap across D1 athletic departments — with thousands of student athletes requiring verifiable, portable identity credentials for endorsement and licensing.
The HAND Standard

Human & Digital founded
HAND. They are distinct entities.

Human & Digital, Inc. is the for-profit media technology company that conceived, developed, and commercializes services built on the HAND identifier ecosystem.

HAND — The Universal Talent Identifier is an independent Delaware non-stock, nonprofit C corporation and 501(c)(6) trade association and standards body. It maintains the identifier registry, sets governance policy, and operates in the public interest of talent across cultural industries.

This structure is intentional. A neutral, nonprofit-governed standard earns broader adoption across competing commercial interests. H&D’s commercial services create the sustainable infrastructure that makes that standard viable in practice.
